# 7. Historian Group

Evaluation pipelines, historical case files, retrospective audits, and performance records.

## EO-061 Historian Group Framework

The Historian Group framework is implemented as ARGOS's scientific evaluation organization. It creates historical cases, evaluates enterprise performance using measurable evidence, rejects anecdotal conclusions, produces validated learning records, and routes advisory recommendations to the Librarian Group, Executive Group, and future Engineering Orders without modifying organizational behavior directly.

## EO-062 Performance Measurement Office

The Performance Measurement Office is implemented as ARGOS's deterministic organizational performance authority. It calculates standardized empirical metrics for Executive, Seeker, Analyst, Risk, and Trader functions, generates organizational scorecards, archives historical performance, identifies long-term trends, preserves metric calculation traces, and produces Librarian-facing measurement standards without influencing active operations.

## EO-063 Hypothesis Validation Office

The Hypothesis Validation Office is implemented as ARGOS's deterministic scientific validation authority. It registers organizational hypotheses, manages lifecycle transitions, correlates evidence, performs falsification analysis, calibrates confidence, detects drift, generates evidence-based learning recommendations, preserves validation archives, and prevents unvalidated hypotheses from progressing toward institutional doctrine.

## EO-064 Model Calibration Office

The Model Calibration Office is implemented as ARGOS's deterministic model optimization authority. It maintains version-controlled model registries, calculates prediction error, detects systematic bias, generates evidence-based calibration recommendations, compares model generations, simulates proposed calibrations, preserves immutable model version history, and produces calibration standards without directly updating active models.

## EO-065 Prompt Evaluation Office

The Prompt Evaluation Office is implemented as ARGOS's deterministic prompt effectiveness authority. It treats prompts as organizational assets and operational hypotheses, maintains prompt version history, measures empirical prompt performance, performs controlled experimentation, benchmarks against historical and human baselines, validates production readiness, produces optimization recommendations, maintains prompt evidence, and generates enterprise prompt analytics without directly modifying prompts.

## EO-066 Decision Evaluation Office

The Decision Evaluation Office is implemented as ARGOS's deterministic authority for evaluating organizational decision quality independently of investment outcomes. It registers major decisions, evaluates reasoning quality, evidence sufficiency, alternative analysis, contradiction handling, governance discipline, recurring patterns, and evidence-based recommendations while preserving immutable historical decision archives.

## EO-067 Evidence Evaluation Office

The Evidence Evaluation Office is implemented as ARGOS's deterministic authority for evaluating organizational evidence independently from conclusions. It registers evidence, preserves provenance, calculates quality, independence, freshness, sufficiency, conflict severity, deterministic evidence weights, integrity records, and evidence-based recommendations while maintaining immutable historical evidence archives.

## EO-068 Historian Fusion Office

The Historian Fusion Office is implemented as the deterministic integration authority for the Historian Group. It aggregates reports from Performance Measurement, Hypothesis Validation, Model Calibration, Decision Evaluation, and Evidence Evaluation offices, detects conflicting evaluations, identifies recurring organizational patterns, generates validated organizational learning, promotes institutional knowledge with provenance, and prepares Librarian and Academy deliverables.

## EO-069 Historian Operational Readiness Review

The Historian Operational Readiness Review is implemented as the deterministic certification authority for the Historian Group. It verifies implementation completeness, interfaces, deterministic historical analysis, workflow execution, empirical integrity, controlled knowledge promotion, adversarial validation, Librarian integration, certification archives, and validated knowledge-transfer authorization.
