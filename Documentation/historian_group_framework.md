# EO-061 Historian Group Framework

## Purpose

The Historian Group is the scientific evaluation organization of ARGOS. It converts operational history into validated institutional knowledge by determining what happened, why it happened, which assumptions were correct, which assumptions failed, and how the enterprise should improve based on measurable evidence.

## Governance Boundaries

The Historian Group does not gather market intelligence, make investment decisions, execute trades, or directly modify organizational behavior. Recommendations are advisory inputs to the Librarian Group, Executive Group, and future Engineering Orders.

## Core Responsibilities

- Collect enterprise performance data from Executive, Seeker, Analyst, Risk, and Trader groups.
- Maintain historical cases for significant organizational actions.
- Evaluate investment returns, decision accuracy, risk prediction accuracy, execution quality, evidence quality, prompt effectiveness, organizational consistency, model performance, process reliability, and learning velocity.
- Validate hypotheses, prompts, models, evidence, and organizational processes against measured outcomes.
- Produce historical evaluations and validated learning records.
- Preserve traceability between historical cases, Executive decisions, organizational evidence, prompts, models, trades, positions, organizational learning, and institutional knowledge.

## Produced Artifacts

- `HISTORICAL_CASE`
- `HISTORICAL_EVALUATION`
- `VALIDATED_LEARNING_RECORD`
- `HistorianSystemPrompt`
- `HistorianGroupArchitecture`
- `OrganizationalPerformanceMetrics`

## Initial Offices

- Historical Case Office
- Performance Evaluation Office
- Hypothesis Validation Office
- Organizational Learning Office

## Scientific Rule

No anecdotal evidence may support Historian conclusions. Every conclusion must be supported by measurable evidence and remain permanently reconstructable from preserved historical records.
