# EO-066 Decision Evaluation Office

## Purpose

The Decision Evaluation Office is ARGOS's deterministic authority for evaluating organizational decision quality independently of investment outcomes. It evaluates the quality of reasoning, evidence, alternatives, and discipline rather than the profitability of resulting trades.

## Core Responsibilities

- Maintain immutable decision registries.
- Evaluate reasoning quality.
- Evaluate evidence sufficiency.
- Evaluate alternative analysis.
- Measure decision discipline and governance compliance.
- Identify recurring decision patterns.
- Generate evidence-based organizational recommendations.
- Preserve historical decision archives and complete auditability.

## Produced Artifacts

- `DECISION_REGISTRY`
- `DECISION_EVALUATION_REPORT`
- `DECISION_PATTERN_REPORT`
- `ORGANIZATIONAL_DECISION_SUMMARY`
- `DECISION_DISCIPLINE_REPORT`
- Reasoning Evaluation Dataset
- Evidence Sufficiency Archive
- Alternative Analysis Archive
- Decision Pattern Register
- Organizational Recommendation Register
- Decision Integrity Assessment

## Deterministic Methodology

Decision quality is calculated from reasoning support, evidence sufficiency, alternative-analysis sufficiency, contradiction handling, and governance discipline. Profitability is explicitly excluded from decision-quality calculations.

## Librarian Deliverables

- Decision Evaluation Standards
- Organizational Decision Methodology
- Governance Evaluation Specifications

## Governance Boundaries

The office evaluates decisions after they occur. It does not make investment decisions, execute trades, alter Executive decisions, or directly modify organizational behavior. Recommendations remain advisory inputs to the Librarian Group, Executive Group, and future Engineering Orders.
