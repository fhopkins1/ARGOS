# EO-070 Librarian Group Framework

## Purpose

The Librarian Group is ARGOS's deterministic institutional knowledge authority. It preserves, governs, versions, organizes, and distributes empirically validated organizational knowledge.

## Core Responsibilities

- Maintain institutional knowledge repositories.
- Classify organizational doctrine.
- Version institutional knowledge.
- Promote validated lessons.
- Preserve historical doctrine.
- Maintain reference integrity.
- Distribute organizational knowledge.
- Preserve complete auditability.

## Produced Artifacts

- `LIBRARIAN_INSTITUTIONAL_KNOWLEDGE_REPORT`
- `DOCTRINE_STATUS_REPORT`
- `REPOSITORY_INTEGRITY_REPORT`
- `ORGANIZATIONAL_KNOWLEDGE_SUMMARY`
- Institutional Knowledge Repository
- Doctrine Registry
- Knowledge Version Archive
- Knowledge Promotion Register
- Reference Graph
- Repository Health Database
- Organizational Standards Archive

## Initial Offices

- Institutional Knowledge Office
- Doctrine Management Office
- Specification Repository Office
- Knowledge Graph Office
- Learning Integration Office
- Librarian Fusion Office

## Doctrine Rule

Only empirically validated knowledge with Historian transfer authorization may become authoritative doctrine. Unauthorized or incomplete knowledge is stored as candidate knowledge and cannot become doctrine until proper validation and authorization exist.

## Governance Boundaries

The Librarian Group preserves and distributes doctrine. It does not create market intelligence, make investment decisions, rewrite historical evidence, or bypass Historian validation. Historical doctrine and knowledge versions remain immutable.
