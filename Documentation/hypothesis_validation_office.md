# EO-063 Hypothesis Validation Office

## Purpose

The Hypothesis Validation Office is ARGOS's deterministic scientific validation authority. It empirically determines whether organizational hypotheses are supported, contradicted, drifting, validated, or rejected by observed reality.

## Core Responsibilities

- Maintain an immutable hypothesis registry.
- Track deterministic hypothesis lifecycles.
- Correlate evidence to hypotheses.
- Actively falsify hypotheses.
- Calibrate organizational confidence.
- Detect hypothesis drift.
- Generate evidence-based organizational learning recommendations.
- Preserve historical validation records and archives.
- Gate institutional doctrine so only validated hypotheses may progress.

## Produced Artifacts

- `HYPOTHESIS_REGISTRY`
- `HYPOTHESIS_VALIDATION_REPORT`
- `HYPOTHESIS_DRIFT_REPORT`
- `ORGANIZATIONAL_HYPOTHESIS_SUMMARY`
- `CONFIDENCE_CALIBRATION_REPORT`
- Validation lifecycle records
- Evidence correlation records
- Falsification records
- Confidence calibration records
- Hypothesis drift records
- Organizational learning recommendations

## Librarian Deliverables

- Validated Hypothesis Archive
- Organizational Validation Standards
- Confidence Calibration Methodology

## Doctrine Gate

Only hypotheses with validated empirical support and no active falsification may progress toward institutional doctrine. Supported, contradicted, drifting, rejected, or inconclusive hypotheses remain outside doctrine until future deterministic validation changes their status.

## Governance Boundaries

The office validates knowledge. It does not rely on anecdotal success, does not make investment decisions, does not execute trades, and does not directly modify organizational behavior.
