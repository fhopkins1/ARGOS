# EO-067 Evidence Evaluation Office

## Purpose

The Evidence Evaluation Office is ARGOS's deterministic authority for evaluating organizational evidence independently from the conclusions drawn from that evidence. Its role is to ensure confidence remains proportional to evidence quality, not opinion, authority, or historical success.

## Core Responsibilities

- Maintain immutable evidence registries.
- Evaluate evidence quality.
- Measure evidence independence.
- Evaluate evidence sufficiency.
- Detect evidence conflicts.
- Measure evidence freshness.
- Calculate deterministic evidence weights.
- Generate evidence-based organizational recommendations.
- Preserve provenance, evidence archives, and auditability.

## Produced Artifacts

- `EVIDENCE_REGISTRY`
- `EVIDENCE_EVALUATION_REPORT`
- `EVIDENCE_CONFLICT_REPORT`
- `EVIDENCE_SUFFICIENCY_REPORT`
- `ORGANIZATIONAL_EVIDENCE_SUMMARY`
- Evidence Quality Dataset
- Evidence Independence Archive
- Evidence Weight Database
- Evidence Freshness Archive
- Evidence Conflict Register
- Provenance Registry
- Evidence Integrity Assessment

## Deterministic Methodology

Evidence quality is calculated from reliability, relevance, and completeness. Evidence independence is based on distinct independence groups. Freshness is calculated from collection time. Evidence weight combines quality, independence, freshness, and conflict penalty through deterministic formulas.

## Librarian Deliverables

- Evidence Evaluation Standards
- Evidence Weighting Methodology
- Provenance Specifications

## Governance Boundaries

The office evaluates evidence, not conclusions. It does not decide whether a trade, hypothesis, model, doctrine, or Executive action is correct. It supplies objective evidence quality records for downstream Historian fusion and institutional learning.
